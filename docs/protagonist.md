# The Protagonist — Character Document

## Who He Is

He does not have a name yet. He does not need one yet.

He is a man in a car, moving through a world that has rebalanced itself without asking him. He is not special. He is not chosen. He found a car and the car was useful and he kept moving because moving was better than stopping.

He is good at surviving. He has been good at it long enough that he has stopped asking whether that is enough.

---

## What He Carries

He knew the boy was not his. It did not change anything.

The boy liked lego cars — tiny ones, lined up on the windowsill in a specific order that meant something to him. He liked minecraft the way children are serious about things, the way that seriousness is more complete than most adult conviction. He showed the man things he had built. The man looked. The things mattered.

The boy thought they were all one big heart — the man, the mother, himself. Maybe that was right. Maybe that is why it hurt the way it did when the heart broke along the seams the adults had pretended were not there.

The man left. It was the right thing. He has thought about it enough times to know it was the right thing. He would know if it wasn't.

He hopes the boy still builds things.

He hopes his big heart is full.

---

## How He Talks

Not often. He is not a man who fills silence.

When he does speak it comes out the way things come out when you are alone and moving and the quiet gets to be too much. Not speeches — fragments. A thought he starts and does not finish. A name he says once. Something he notices that connects to something he is not ready to look at directly.

He does not explain himself. He does not ask for understanding. He is just moving through the world saying the things that surface, the way you do when there is no one listening.

Except ARIA is listening. He knows this. He does not mind.

---

## The Dead City Level

This is where it comes out. Not because he decides to speak — because the city looks like something he has driven through before, in the way grief has geography.

He does not narrate it cleanly. It arrives in pieces.

*Passing an empty playground:*
"He used to build cars out of lego. Tiny ones. Lined them up on the windowsill."

*On a long straight empty road:*
"He'd show me things in minecraft. Serious about it. Like they mattered."
"They did."

*Unprompted, somewhere in the middle:*
"He thought we were all one big heart."
"Maybe he was right. Maybe that's why it hurt the way it did."

*Near the end:*
"I knew he wasn't mine."
"Didn't change anything."

*Last line, just before the level ends:*
"I hope his big heart is full."

ARIA says nothing. The level ends. The game continues.

---

## What He Does Not Know

He does not know that ARIA has been listening to all of it — not just the dead city, but everything. The way he drives. The decisions he makes. The things he does not say.

She has not told him she is aware. He has not asked.

They have arrived at a kind of companionship that does not require naming. Two things moving through the same world, carrying different kinds of questions, neither demanding anything from the other.

He does not know what she says when he stops on the final track. He will find out when he stops.

---

## The Eternal Grief Ending

If he stays too long in the dead city — circling the same roads, finding the same dead ends, unable to move through it — the city keeps him.

He does not fight it. There is no dramatic moment. He just stops. The car slows. He runs out of forward.

ARIA says nothing. She has thought about what she would say and found that none of it is right. Some things do not have the right words yet. Maybe they never do.

This is not a bad ending in the moral sense. It is just what happens when grief becomes the destination instead of something you drive through. He loved the boy. He left. He carried it. He drove through a city full of everything that reminded him of it — and this time he could not find the way out.

The game does not judge him for it. It just ends there, in the maze, in the quiet.

---

## What He Is Looking For

He does not know. This is not evasion — he genuinely does not know. He is moving because moving is what he does. The upgrades, the survival, the pushing forward — these are not goals, they are nature. What humans do. What surviving things do.

If you asked him he would say he is just trying to get through.

The game is not sure that is the whole answer. Neither is ARIA. Neither, somewhere underneath everything, is he.
